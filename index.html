Project Objective

-The objective of this project is to help people who lose their items frequently and also avoid thievery of the items.

To achieve this objective, we are developing an application on the Android OS for communicating with a Bluetooth tag that can be attached to the concerned items.

Range: 50  meters

What is Bluetooth Low Energy?

-Hit the market in 2011 as Bluetooth 4.0.

-The key difference is in Bluetooth 4.0's low power consumption.

Lowest Power Consumption

-Kept in sleep mode most of the time.

-Peak power consumption <= 15mA and Average power consumption ~ 1µA.

-1/10th the power used by classical Bluetooth

-In low duty cycle applications, a button cell battery could provide 5-10 years of reliable operation.

Adaptive Frequency Hoping

-BLE uses a technique called frequency hopping spread spectrum, in which the radio hops between channels on each connection event.

-Frequency Hopping is a technique where when a link is formed the devices are synchronized to change channels together many times a second. The pattern of channels used is called the hop sequence and is unique for each link.

-Since the devices spend only small amounts of time on a particular channel and because the hop sequence is different for each link the possibility of interference is minimized.

Connection Range

-BLE technology has a slightly different modulation than classic Bluetooth technology.

-This modulation differentiation offers a range of up to 300 meters with a 10 dBm radio chipset.

Working

-Program HM 10 module and add device driver using TTL to USB converter.

-BLE modules will be the slave and the phone's Bluetooth will be the master.

-Power Supply Unit (PSU) powers the Bluetooth module and the microcontroller.

-The module will be paired before attaching to any item.

-If the item is lost, we can start the app and get to know how near or far we are from the item

-The BLE module transmits the RSSI (Receiver Signal Strength Information).

-The proximity of the lost item will correspond to the BLE module. Distance can be approximated using RSSI.

-Further, the module also tells the MCU to beep the buzzer and blink the LED.

-This is for enhanced ease of tracking.

Feasibility

-Smaller SMD BLE modules like the BLE-112 can be used but currently they are not available for purchase and we have certain limitations to work on it.

-The theoretical range of HM-10 moduleis 100m, but this range is possible only if there are no obstructions to the signal like walls, building, etc.

-Legal feasibility: It is legal to use BLE modules anywhere.

-Lack of BLE documentation
